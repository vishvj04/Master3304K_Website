STEP by STEP

The Approach

BRAINSTORM

At the beginning of the season, our team sets aside a couple of weeks to meet, and brainstorm design ideas by analyzing the game, and looking over the rules. We normally take a look at the scoring breakdown, and the game objects in order to figure out which design will be most compatible, and smooth with the game objects. We then decide on a specific strategy we would like to implement, and a list of mechanisms we want for the robot.

We then protoype these designs separately, with a chosen strategy in mind. We build these mechanisms separately with the objective to see whether they fit our purpose, and the strategy we have in mind. If not, then the ideology has to be changed.

BUILD

Using the protoypes that worked most successfully, we build a basic robot using the robot designs that we had previously decided on. This robot is not a competition bot, but is rather just a beta testing bot, which we use to run the mechanisms and view how successfull they are once it gets to real time situtations. Although we might take it to early season competitions, this is not our fully functioning final robot. We use this robot to build off of, and see where major fixes must be implemented. This step is vital, as it gives us the knowledge we require to make changes to the smaller issues before they develop.

Finally, we take this beta testing robot, and take the improvements that can be made to build a fully functioning competition robot. We do this using V5 motors, and the unproblematic mechnisms that we developed during earlier stages. This gives us the 'first iteration robot', which we will not only build on, but will use for competition.

IMPROVE

From here, we make continuous changes and improvements based on where we faltered during competition, and where we believe we could've done better. This may mean smaller tweaks, such as the replacement of motors, or addition of a major component, such as an arm, or intake. For example, if we realize that the foundation, and fundamentals of our robot are flawed in any way, rebuild and redesign would be the best option. This doesn't necessarily mean we return to where we started, but rather we continue to build on prior ideas, as we already have a good understanding of the game, and objectives by this point.
